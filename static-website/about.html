About Professor Pritam Singh

Professor Pritam Singh

Emeritus Professor of Economics

Oxford Brookes University, Oxford, UK

Biography

Professor Pritam Singh is Emeritus Professor of Economics at Oxford Brookes Business School (awarded Emeritus status 2018). His expertise spans development economics, environmental economics, comparative economic systems, and the political economy of human rights and nationalism, particularly related to India and Punjab. He leads the research cluster on Environmental and Development Economics at Oxford Brookes University. His work focuses on the sustainability implications of the spatial shift in global capitalism and Indian capitalism with emphasis on decentralisation and human rights.

Doctoral Supervision

Actively involved in doctoral student supervision as Director of Studies. Has supervised three PhDs to successful completion (Michael Ochurub, 2001; V.K. Mensah, 2008; Lok Nath Bhusal, 2012) and is currently supervising seven PhD students as Director of Studies, including one from Burgundy Business School, Dijon and one by publication route. Has externally examined PhDs for universities in the UK, Ireland, India and Pakistan. Also on the supervisory/examination committee of a PhD student at the State University of New Jersey, Rutgers.
